The Problem With Enterprise Training

For decades we’ve worked with large enterprises on their transformation initiatives. Training has proven foundational to each of them. It’s unrealistic to ask people to work in a radically new way without clearly explaining how work will get done. However, with large enterprises we consistently run into the same 3 problems.

Problem 1: Scalability

The traditional approach with instructor-led training is not designed for the speed or scale needed. Seldom are there enough trainers or classes to keep pace with demand. Put simply, instructor-led training doesn’t scale.

Problem 2. Flexibility

With ever escalating demands on employees, few enterprises can afford to sideline their workers for a two-day training session. Instructor-led training forces a bargain. Either neglect role responsibilities or opt out of training. Neither option serves the enterprise well.

Problem 3. Efficacy

The human brain is quite adept at shelving any information it doesn’t deem immediately relevant. The time between learning a new concept and its application is critical. In a traditional training model, this lag time can amount to weeks or months.
